pH based crops

Certainly! Soil pH is a crucial factor that influences the types of crops that can be grown. Here's a detailed breakdown of suitable crops for different soil pH ranges, focusing on India:
● pH 1-2: This range is highly acidic and generally not suitable for crop cultivation.
● pH 2-3: Extremely acidic soils can support very few crops; however, some acid-tolerant plants may survive.
● pH 3-4: Acidic soils in this range can support crops like tea, rubber, and certain varieties of rice and potatoes.
● pH 4-5: Suitable crops include rice, soybean, wheat, peanut, pea, and potato.
● pH 5-6: This range can accommodate a wider variety of crops, including rice, soybean, wheat, beetroot, corn, and sweet potato4.
● pH 6-7: Slightly acidic to neutral soils are ideal for most crops. Crops like kale, lettuce, sweet peas, pole beans, cole crops (cabbage, Brussels sprouts, broccoli), and radishes thrive in this range3.
● pH 7-8: Neutral to slightly alkaline soils can support crops like barley, wheat, and certain legumes.
● pH 8-9: Alkaline soils can support crops like spinach, beets, and certain types of beans.
● pH 9-10: Highly alkaline soils are challenging for most crops, but some salt-tolerant varieties may be suitable.
It's important to note that the above information is a general guideline, and specific crop suitability can vary based on local conditions, soil type, and management practices. Additionally, soil amendments can be used to adjust the pH to suit the requirements of different crops. For optimal crop production, it's essential to regularly test soil pH and make necessary adjustments. If you need more specific information or have other questions, feel free to ask!
